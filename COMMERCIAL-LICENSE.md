## When you need a Commercial License

Consider a Commercial License if any of the following apply:

- You want to **embed Hypratia Core into a closed-source product** without releasing your modifications under AGPLv3.
- You operate Hypratia Core as a **network service** for customers and cannot comply with AGPLv3 §13 (the source-code-disclosure requirement for remote network interaction).
- You need **commercial-use support, indemnification, or a service-level agreement**.
- You are deploying Hypratia in an **Enterprise** environment (SSO, audit logging, multi-tenant administration, on-prem support).
- You want access to **Pro features** that are not part of Hypratia Core.

If none of the above apply, AGPLv3 is sufficient and no commercial agreement is required.

## FAQ

**Q. I want to fork Hypratia Core and run it for myself. Do I need a Commercial License?**
No. AGPLv3 covers personal and internal use. You only need to comply with AGPLv3's terms (e.g., publishing your source modifications if you offer the modified version as a network service).

**Q. Can I rename my fork "Hypratia-X" or use the logo?**
No. The name and logo are out of scope for AGPLv3. Pick a different name and use your own branding.

**Q. We're a non-profit / academic lab. Does that change anything?**
AGPLv3 already permits your use. If you want Pro features or support specifically, contact us — we sometimes offer reduced terms for non-commercial deployments.

**Q. Is this dual-licensing?**
Yes. Hypratia Core is available under AGPLv3 by default; the Commercial License is an alternative grant for cases where AGPLv3 does not fit.
